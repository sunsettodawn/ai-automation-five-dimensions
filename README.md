The Five Dimensions of Artificial Intelligence for Automation Model

Abstract
This paper introduces The Five Dimensions of Artificial Intelligence for Automation Model, a practical framework for structuring, assessing and de-risking complex AI automation initiatives. Rather than approaching automation as a purely technological exercise, the model proposes a broader perspective built around five interdependent dimensions: Process, Data, Technology, Integration and Action, and Viability and Ethics. Together, these dimensions provide a disciplined way to evaluate whether an AI automation project is not only technically feasible, but also operationally resilient, economically justified, and governable in practice.

The framework is designed for organizations seeking to move beyond experimentation and toward reliable implementation. It supports decision-making in early project stages by helping teams identify process bottlenecks, map uncertainty and edge cases, assess data readiness, select fit-for-purpose architectures, define safe action boundaries, and establish clear go/no-go criteria before deployment. In this sense, the model serves both as a strategic planning tool and as an execution support framework for cross-functional teams.

This paper is intended for business sponsors, process owners, risk and compliance leaders, architects, and technical practitioners involved in AI-enabled transformation. Its value lies in offering a common language across business, operations, technology and governance, making it easier to align stakeholders, reduce avoidable project failure, and prepare AI automation initiatives with greater confidence, traceability and institutional trust.
